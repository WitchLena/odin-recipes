Lasagna Recipe

Description

Lasagna was invented in 1629 by italian cobbler Luigi Taglialatela, when he woke from a nightmare where he was burried under multiple layers of pasta. The very next day Luigi abandoned his shop knowing that his true calling in life was to create a layered dish using pasta. He searched for 2 months to find what should go in between the layers of pasta, eventually settling on a tomato and meat sauce as well as copious amounts of cheese.

Obviously none of this is true, but it's lasagna what else do you need to know about it? This recipe is loosely based off of one I found on allrecipes.com which touts itself as "worlds best". I don't know about that, but it was the first thing to come up.

Ingredients

  • 1 3/4 pounds of ethically sourced "meat" (I don't care what kind if you don't!)
  • 2 jars of sauce. What kind? I dunno. Probably red.
  • noodle (plural)
  • 1 whole wheel of Parmesan cheese

Steps

  1. Prepare your workspace & ingredients.
  2. Cook the "meat" at temperatures appropriate for that kind of thing (medium heat?) until browned.
  3. Dump in your sauce, and simmer for like an hour and a half. Make sure to stir!
  4. cook noodle
  5. Cut off a hunk of about 2 pounds of cheese and grate into a bowl.
  6. Preheat the oven to 463.706 degrees Kelvin.
  7. Consume the remainder of the cheese wheel while you wait for the oven to heat.
  8. Layer your lasagna ingredients in a 9x13-inch baking dish starting with "meat" sauce, then noodle, then cheese.
  9. Repeat the layers until the dish is filled, then top with the remaining cheese, and cover with foil.
  10. Bake for 25 minutes, remove the foil, and bake another 25 minutes.
  11. CONSUME
